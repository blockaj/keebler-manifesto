A Treatise on Why Keebler Will Fail

by Karl Marx
(annotated by Aaron Block)

Introduction

We all know Keebler makes delicious cookies; the best in fact. Did you know that the reason Keebler cookies are so delicious is because they are made by real elves using real elven magic? Karl Marx did. In his Treatise on Why Keebler Will Fail, Marx addresses the structural flaws with the Keebler cookie company.

Section I

The secret behind Keebler's enticing treats are the laborers' use of elven magic. Of course, elven magic is a scarce resource. Furthermore, elven magic that will improve the taste of cookies is even more scarce. Therefore, Keebler cannot easily trade laborers. Their laborers are the only ones who can produce such tasty products. Keebler Elves cannot be commodified like other laborers due to their specialized skill.

"These labourers, who must sell themselves piecemeal, are a commodity" - Communist Manifesto

Section II

I've said before that specialized skill does not protect the jobs of the proletariat. Companies with huge resources can easily divide labor to a point of simplicity such that anyone can accomplish the task. At this point a big company can begin to commodify their laborers again. This cannot happen at Keebler. Keebler Elves have a skill that is intrinsic to them: making cookies. Their magic cannot be replicated and therefore, dividing labor among them does not help in commodifying the labor.

"...their specialised skill is rendered worthless by new methods of production." - Communist Manifesto

Section III

Related to the commodification of labor, there is the issue of "means of production." Since Keebler Elves possess the magic that makes the cookies so good they, in essence, own the means of production. Keebler elves are treated like the proletariat by all measures. They work like the proletariat, live like the proletariat and are payed like the proletariat. However, like the bourgeoisie, they own the means of production. Without the elves, it is impossible for Keebler to make a profit. Therefore, Keebler elves have all the negotiating power and the company has none. What is to stop the elves from taking all of the profit?

"Society as a whole is more and more splitting up into two great hostile camps, into two great classes directly facing each other ‐ Bourgeoisie and Proletariat." - Communist Manifesto

Section IV

This scenario perfectly explains the true definition of the bourgeoisie. The bourgeoisie has little to do with how much money one makes or how they are treated. The bourgeoisie is defined by what one owns. The bourgeoisie owns means of production: capital, materials, and tools to make profit. Since people buy Keebler cookies because they are so delicious and the elven magic is what makes the cookies so delicious, the Keebler elves own the means of production. This makes the Keebler elves the bourgeoisie.

"If we examine the whole process from the point of view of its result, the product, it is plain that both the instruments and the subject of labour, are means of production, and that the labour itself is productive labour." - Das Kapital "

Section V

Who is to tell the Keebler elves how much they should be paid? They are the reason that the company makes any money at all. It would be easy for them to go somewhere else and make delicious cookies for another company if they are unsatisfied with the pay that Keebler gives them.
