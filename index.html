A Treatise on Why Keebler Will Fail

by Karl Marx
(annotated by Aaron Block)

Introduction

We all know Keebler makes delicious cookies; the best in fact. Did you know that the reason Keebler cookies are so delicious is because they are made by real elves using real elven magic? Karl Marx did. In his Treatise on Why Keebler Will Fail, Marx addresses the structural flaws with the Keebler cookie company.

Reason I

The secret behind Keebler's enticing treats are the laborers' use of elven magic. Of course, elven magic is a scarce resource. Furthermore, elven magic that will improve the taste of cookies is even more scarce. Therefore, Keebler cannot easily trade laborers. Their laborers are the only ones who can produce such tasty products. Keebler Elves cannot be commodified like other laborers due to their specialized skill.

"These labourers, who must sell themselves piecemeal, are a commodity" - Communist Manifesto

Reason II

I've said before that specialized skill does not protect the jobs of the proletariat. Companies with huge resources can easily divide labor to a point of simplicity such that anyone can accomplish the task. At this point a big company can begin to commodify their laborers again. This cannot happen at Keebler. Keebler Elves have a skill that is intrinsic to them: making cookies. Their magic cannot be replicated and therefore, dividing labor among them does not help in commodifying the labor.

"...their specialised skill is rendered worthless by new methods of production." - Communist Manifesto
